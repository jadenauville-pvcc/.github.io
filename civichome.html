US Title Code 18

    United States Code Title 18 is the main criminal code of the federal government of the United States. This title deals with federal crimes and criminal procedure. It consists of five different parts: Crimes, Criminal Procedure, Prisons and Prisoners, Correction of Youthful Offenders, and Immunity of Witnesses.

    Part 1: Crimes

    • This part primarily deals with crimes. It is a significant portion of federal criminal law, covering a wide range of offenses, their definitions, penalties, and procedures for prosecution. This part is divided into chapters that each focus on specific categories of crimes and related matters. Some chapters included are Chapter 2: Aircraft and Motor Vehicles, Chapter 7: Assault, Chapter 25: Counterfeiting and Forgery, and Chapter 113: Stolen Property.
    • Chapter 2: Aircraft and Motor Vehicles: This chapter addresses crimes involving aircraft and motor vehicles, including hijacking, destruction of aircraft, interference with flight crew members, and transportation of stolen vehicles.
    • Chapter 7: Assault: This chapter encompasses various offenses related to assault, including aggravated assault, assault with intent to commit a felony, and assault on federal officers or employees.
    • Chapter 25: Counterfeiting and Forgery: This chapter encompasses offenses related to counterfeiting currency, securities, and other documents, as well as forgery of government seals and official insignia.
    • Chapter 113: Stolen Property: This chapter covers offenses related to the receipt, possession, and transportation of stolen property, as well as trafficking in stolen goods.

    Part 2: Criminal Procedure

    • Part II, primarily deals with criminal procedure. It outlines the rules and procedures that govern the investigation, prosecution, and adjudication of criminal cases in federal courts. Part II is essential for ensuring the fair and orderly administration of justice within the federal criminal justice system. Some of the chapters included in part 2 are Chapter 205: Searches and Seizures, Chapter 207: Release and Detention Pending Judicial Proceedings, Chapter 211: Jurisdiction and Venue, and Chapter 217: Witnesses and Evidence. These chapters, along with others within Part 2, provide the procedural framework for investigating, prosecuting, and adjudicating criminal cases in federal courts. They ensure that the rights of defendants, victims, and other parties are protected and that criminal proceedings are conducted fairly and efficiently.
    • Chapter 205: Searches and Seizures: This chapter addresses the Fourth Amendment protections against unreasonable searches and seizures by law enforcement officers. It establishes rules for obtaining search warrants, conducting searches, and seizing evidence in criminal investigations.
    • Chapter 207: Release and Detention Pending Judicial Proceedings: This chapter covers procedures for pretrial release and detention of defendants awaiting trial, including bail hearings and conditions of release.
    • Chapter 211: Jurisdiction and Venue: This chapter defines the jurisdiction of federal courts over criminal cases and establishes rules for determining the proper venue for prosecution.
    • Chapter 217: This chapter addresses the rules for subpoenaing witnesses, presenting evidence, and conducting testimony in criminal trials and other judicial proceedings.

    Part 3: Prisons and Prisoners

    • Part 3 primarily deals specifically with "Prisons and Prisoners." This part contains several chapters, each addressing different aspects related to the management, treatment, and rights of prisoners within the federal system. Some of the chapters included are Chapter 303: Bureau of Prisons, Chapter 305: Commitment and Transfer, and Chapter 315: Discharge and Release Payments. These chapters collectively provide a framework for the management, treatment, and rights of prisoners within the federal system as outlined in Title 18 of the United States Code.
    • Chapter 303: Bureau of Prisons: This chapter focuses on the Bureau of Prisons, which is the federal agency responsible for the administration of federal prisons and detention centers. It likely details the organization, functions, and powers of the Bureau of Prisons, as well as its responsibilities in managing federal inmates.
    • Chapter 305: Commitment and Transfer: This chapter deals with the procedures for the commitment and transfer of prisoners within the federal system. It may include provisions regarding the legal processes involved in sentencing, transferring prisoners between facilities, and managing interstate prisoner transfers.
    • Chapter 315: Discharge and Release Payments: This chapter may cover procedures for the discharge and release of prisoners from federal custody, as well as any payments or obligations owed to state and local officials upon the release of federal prisoners into their jurisdictions.

    Part 4: Correction of Youthful Offenders

    • Part 4 pertains to "Corrections of Youthful Offenders." This part addresses issues related to the treatment and rehabilitation of individuals who have committed crimes while they were juveniles or young adults.

    Part 5: Immunity of Witnesses

    • Part V addresses immunity for witnesses in federal criminal proceedings. It outlines procedures for granting immunity to witnesses to compel their testimony in cases where their testimony is deemed essential to the administration of justice.
